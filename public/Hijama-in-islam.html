Hijama In Islam

(Hijama) is a therapeutic practice rooted in the sunnah of Prophet Muhammad (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam). He not only personally underwent hijama but also advocated its efficacy as a beneficial treatment for various ailments.

It's crucial to note that while different forms and methods of cupping offer benefits, the specific practice of wet cupping (HIJAMA) aligns with the prophetic tradition.

Here are narrations from the sunnah of Prophet Muhammad (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) emphasizing the significance of HIJAMA therapy:

Anas bin Malik (radiyallahu anhu) reported that during the night journey (Lailatul Israa), whenever the Prophet (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) passed by gatherings of angels, they would instruct him: "Oh Muhammad, enjoin your community to embrace Cupping (as a form of treatment)." (Sunan Ibn Majah 3479)

Ibn Abbas narrated that during the night journey (Lailatul Israa), the Messenger of Allah (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) encountered assemblies of angels, and each one advised him: "Oh Muhammad, consider Cupping (have cupping performed)." (Sunan Ibn Majah 3477)

Narrated Jabir (radiyallahu anhu), Umm Salah (wife of the Prophet Sallallahu alaihi wasallam), sought permission from the Messenger of Allah (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) to undergo Cupping. Consequently, the Prophet (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) instructed Abu Taibah to perform the cupping. I presumed Abu Taibah to be either her foster brother or a young boy who had not reached puberty. (Sunan Ibn Majah 3480)

Jabir (radiyallahu anhu), upon returning to Muqanna, declared his determination to undergo Cupping, expressing his conviction in the Prophet's (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) statement: "Indeed, there is a cure in it (Cupping)." (Sahih Muslim 2205)

Ibn Abbas (radiyallahu anhu) reported the Prophet (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) saying: "There is a cure in three things: the incisions of the cupper, consuming honey, and cauterization with fire. However, I prohibit my nation from cauterization." (Sahih Al-Bukhari 5681)

Ibn Abbas further narrated that the Prophet (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) received Cupping treatment on his head while in Ihram due to a migraine. (Sahih Al-Bukhari)

Anas (radiyallahu anhu) reported that the Prophet (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) regularly underwent Cupping on the two jugular veins and the upper part of his back, specifically on the 17th, 19th, and 21st days. (Jami at-Tirmidhi 2051)

Anas ibn Malik (radiyallahu anhu) reported that the Messenger of Allah (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) advised those seeking Cupping to choose the 17th, 19th, or 21st days, cautioning against excessive bloodletting to the point of endangering one's life. (Sunan Ibn Majah 3486)

Jabir (radiyallahu anhu) narrated that the Prophet of Allah (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) underwent Cupping on his hip following a sprain or bruise. (Sunan Abu Dawud 3863)

Narrated Jabir (radiyallahu anhu) that the Prophet (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) fell off his horse onto a tree trunk, resulting in a dislocated foot. Waki explained that the Prophet was treated with Cupping on the affected area due to the contusion. (Sunan Ibn Majah 3485)

Salma (radiyallahu anha), the servant of the Messenger of Allah (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam), reported that whenever someone complained to the Prophet about a headache, he would recommend Cupping. Similarly, for leg pain, he advised dyeing them with Henna. (Sunan Abu Dawud 3858)

Upon returning to Muqanna', Jabir (radiyallahu anhu) asserted his commitment to undergoing Cupping, recalling the Prophet's (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) statement about the cure found in it. (Sahih Muslim 2205)

Ibn Abbas (radiyallahu anhu) narrated that the Prophet (Sallallahu alaihi wasallam) received Cupping treatment while fasting. (Sahih Al-Bukhari 5694)
